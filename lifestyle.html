All You Need To Know About Intermittent Fasting

2 WEEKS AGO by CAROL FERRAO 7 min read

Is intermittent fasting a fad or a life-changing solution to lose weight and improve your health? We found the answers for you. Out here in the inter-webs, there is plenty of click-baits guaranteeing weight loss with easy-to-follow diets or even no diets at all. In this sea of (mis)information, we don’t know what to believe. Intermittent Fasting is one such term that’s generating plenty of buzz lately and if youve been wondering what the deal is…

here is our complete intermittent fasting guide for beginners.

So, what is intermittent fasting?

Simply put, it is giving your body a break from eating and allowing it to use stored fat for energy by introducing an eating and fasting window in the 24 hours of a day. When we eat, our body converts the food into energy and stores the excess as fat for future energy needs. Our not-so-active lifestyles require far less energy than we consume and when we eat throughout the day, we are converting more food as fat than burning it. By following an intermittent ‘diet’ or fasting schedule, you allow your body to optimally process the food you eat everyday and avoid the risk of being overweight. As the name ‘intermittent’ suggests, it is about breaking your day in two halves: Eating window: For example, eating all your meals between 9 am – 5 pm* (total of 8 hours per day), Fasting window: Avoiding food from 5 pm until 9 am next morning (16 hours, including your sleeping hours) *Note: The exact timing can vary. With our Intermittent Fasting 101, you can find out why and how you should go about it.

Some of you must be wondering, “Intermittent fasting meaning starvation, right?”

Primarily, people go on intermittent fasting for weight loss. It is an inexpensive way to manage weight and body fat composition without making big changes to your diet or buying pricey supplements and detox drinks. However, we don’t need to limit intermittent fasting for fat loss alone.

You can choose Intermittent fasting plan to:


Maintain healthy insulin and blood sugar levels

Reverse Type 2 Diabetes

Improve energy and stamina levels

Reduce inflammation

Improve cholesterol levels

Boost immunity

Enhance mental clarity

Improve skin conditions

Who should go on an intermittent fasting schedule? IF is followed by people looking to lose weight, reverse diabetes or dealing with chronic illnesses. Plenty of intermittent fasting before and after stories online point out the drastic improvements people have witnessed by adopting this simple measure. However, anyone looking to get healthy and improve their life can follow IF. To be safe and if you have a specific health condition, consult your physician before going on an IF schedule.

Amongst all kinds of tea, green tea leaves go through the least amount of oxidation process, and retains most of the original properties of the fresh tea leaves. On the other hand, black tea leaves are made to oxidise completely, transforming most of the catechins (the compound responsible for weight loss), into tannins which contribute to its distinct flavour.

The catechins preserved in the green tea leaves, specifically epigallocatechin gallate (EGCG), contribute to weight loss by boosting metabolism i.e. your body’s ability to burn fat. These catechins are also responsible for flushing out toxins from the body. Another compound that contributes to weight loss is caffeine. Did you know that a cup of green tea has a quarter of the caffeine content than a cup of coffee? Green tea has just the right amount of caffeine to stimulate your exercise performance and help burn fat better, without inducing an superficial burst of energy that coffee gives (and takes away!).
